Appeals

Being convicted of a criminal offence profoundly impacts one's life, affecting self-esteem, community reputation, employment prospects, travel opportunities and places immense stress on family relationships. Nevertheless, this can be something other than the outcome, as judges, can sometimes make mistakes.

Richard, an experienced appeal lawyer, has a proven track record of successfully challenging decisions in both the Superior Court of Justice (Summary Conviction Appeals) and the Ontario Court of Appeal (Indictable Appeals).

Individuals seeking to appeal their conviction, sentence, or both can arrange a meeting with Richard to explore available options. Potential avenues for an appeal include:

  • Pointing out errors of law, such as instances where the judge misunderstood the evidence or made mistakes regarding the admissibility of evidence.
  • Identifying instances of miscarriage of justice.
  • If you represented yourself, arguing that the judge failed to adequately assist you in presenting your defence during the trial.
  • Regarding the sentence, contending that the judge did not sufficiently consider factors in your favour, imposed an excessive monetary penalty, or handed down an unreasonably long sentence.
